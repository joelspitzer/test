An example of just how disruptive nicotine pouches can be

The following video discusses a post from the QuittingZyn support board on Reddit, illustrating just how disruptive nicotine pouches can be and how difficult it can be to get help from medical professionals for the problems they cause.

Original Post: Day 3 – This experience is incredible

Been a nicotine addict for 10 years – first smoking cigs, then started using 10mg VELO pouches (similar to Zyn) while in the office... 14 months ago I quit smoking and went full-on with the VELO pouches (roughly 15-20 pouches a day of 10mg) and since then, my health has significantly deteriorated.

8-10 months ago I came very close to fainting all of a sudden. Weeks later this happened again, and I started feeling heart palpitations, dizziness, difficulty breathing, difficulty sleeping, restlessness… Went to the doctor who checked EKG, bloodwork, all looked superb. Doc came to the conclusion that I was suffering from anxiety or a similar mental illness, as all my physicals were on point.

Then, on a flight home 4 days ago, I started getting crazy palpitations. I decided to ditch the pouches. This is now ~60 hours ago, and my heart palpitations are COMPLETELY gone. I feel I can breathe 2x the volume of air as I could before. Yesterday I had the best sleep of my entire life.

Follow-up Correspondence

Joel: Glad you got the three days under your belt. I just shot a video response to address your specific experience. Did you ever tell your doctors about the pouches?

Original Poster: No, I actually never told my doctors about VELO. They were aware of my smoking. At the time the thought never crossed my mind that it might be the nicotine... for a good while I actually thought nicotine was a good performance enhancer without any drawbacks.

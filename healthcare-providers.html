Why Informing Your Healthcare Providers of Your Nicotine Pouch Use Can Be Crucial in Getting Proper Diagnoses and Treatment

As nicotine pouches like Zyn, Velo and On! grow in popularity, a troubling lack of awareness about their potential health risks has emerged. This knowledge gap affects not only the general public but also many healthcare professionals, creating a challenging situation for those experiencing adverse effects from these products.

Nicotine pouches can cause a range of health issues, including elevated blood pressure, increased heart rate, and exacerbation of mental health problems like anxiety and depression. Users may also experience intestinal disturbances and other health complications. When individuals face these problems, they often turn to medical professionals for help. However, many doctors, including primary care doctors and emergency room physicians, are unfamiliar with nicotine pouches and their effects.

This lack of awareness can lead to misdiagnoses and inappropriate treatments. In some cases, severe symptoms caused by nicotine pouches, such as panic attacks, may drive individuals to seek emergency care. Unfortunately, ER doctors may not think to ask about nicotine pouch use, and even if informed, they might not understand the specific health issues these products can cause.

To address this problem, patients need to be proactive in their healthcare. It’s crucial to inform all healthcare providers about nicotine pouch use and to be prepared to discuss potential side effects. If a doctor seems unaware of these products, patients should encourage them to research nicotine pouches for a better understanding.

By increasing awareness and encouraging open communication between patients and all types of healthcare providers, we can better address the health risks associated with nicotine pouches. This collaborative approach will not only benefit individual patients but also contribute to a broader understanding of these products within the medical community.
